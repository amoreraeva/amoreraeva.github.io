Neuroscience-based Teaching

Principles

  • To start with ice-breakers to help students relax and feel confident speaking and asking quesions
  • To create relaxed and playful environment inspiring postivie emotions & laughter, self-expression & collaboration
  • To give an opportunity for the students to present themselves and tell more about their interests, expectations from the course, mostly used programs and what they want to learn most
  • To make an overview of everything that will be covered during the course already in the first session & to review it each time
  • To make something practical for each of the topics already in the first lesson and to add on this every time, aiming to have something useful at the end
  • To ask for feedback regularly
  • To provide individual feedback regularly
  • To design the program in such a way, that they can remember the main issues during the class and progress even without studing and practicing at home
  • To inspire them start something during the class that they will be eager to continue at home
  • To suggest homework, further reading & educational resources, including e-books, audio, video & quizzes
  • To encourage them to make notes during the course, to teach them possibles ways to share them and collaborate on revisions
  • Give the students opportunities to present and teach
  • Encourage students to show and share what they have done
